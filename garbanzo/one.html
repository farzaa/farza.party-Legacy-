Track Review
Artist: King Theta
Song: I Wish I Could Start Over [Listen]
Score: A-
We all listen to music for different reasons, but it's almost always because we want to feel something. That feeling could be anything: happiness, excitement, sadness, etc. On his track I Wish I Could Start Over", King Theta puts his audience into this very somber yet reflective mood as he lyricizes about death and desolation.
The track begins with a faint guitar riff that makes the listener feel as if they have their ear to a concrete wall trying to make out a sound, where on the other side the artist is playing the riff. It was this intense curiosity that had me extremely invested in the song just 6 seconds in.
The muffled, melancholic riff becomes clearer when the opener transitions to King Theta singing over it. His vocal style isn't good in the traditional sense. He isn't hitting notes like Adele or singing with beautiful melodies like Frank Ocean. But, he doesn't have to. His vocal style is extremely raw, brutally honest, and oddly addicting. From the first lyric where he gloomily sings "I just wanna feel like I could fly", I was immediately put into this extremely somber and reflective mood.
The core theme of this track is King Theta's obsession with his own desolation and destruction. He screams lyrics like "shit's gonna cave in soon" and "if I die do I get a fresh start". He's fighting between giving up on life and continuing the fight as he beckons for any bit of support he can grab with lyrics like "Baby, tell me imma be a star" and "save me, think I'm gonna fall apart".
The most interesting part about this track for me is that King Theta constantly sings about his personal confidence but really how this is all just a facade for how broken he actually feels inside. Lyrics like "I swear to fuckin God I'm doin' fine" and "Sorry, not sorry for my ego" constantly clash with "I need to fill the void in my heart" and "Hate me so I can stay in the dark". It reminds me of myself and many of my friends in lots ways, where we're extremely proud and push people away - but there is fair bit of tragedy wrapped up under many layers. King Theta surfaces that sort of tragedy on this track.
King Theta's powerful singing is complemented beautifully by an extremely addictive beat that can best be described as punk rock/alternative rock meets a modern-day trap rap beat. It's extremely reminiscent of beats that the late Lil Peep used on tracks that made him a superstar. I think many would actually say that King Theta sounds a lot like Lil Peep or Juice Wrld who both popularized this style of modern "emo" music with hip hop elements. But, I think King Theta is very much forging his own path. I've been listening to the "emo" genre (or whatever we want to call it) for many years now and have a heard a lot of cookie cutter musicians who take a trap beat, write some "sad" lyrics, and record the track with some nasally vocals.
King Theta is more than that. He beautifully crafts these melodramatic lyrics, sings them with real passion, and wraps up the package with a beat that matches the power of his vocals.
